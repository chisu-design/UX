Project Portfolio

Portfolio is not just a way to present who I am. It’s a way to showcase what I can do as an UX designer. I conducted interviews and surveys with professionals in the UX team at MasterCard to have an in-depth understanding of what a professional and attracting UX portfolio should be like. I build a portfolio to truly deliver on what the industry needs.


Research

Questionnaire

The purpose of the survey is to find out what a senior UX designer expects to see in a junior UX’s portfolio when hiring. The questionnaire includes questions about content, medium, visualization, skill sets, experience, quantity of projects, and their advices about how to break into the industry. Google form was used by some of the UX designers from Mastercard to fill out this form.

Interview

I interviewed three senior UX designers who have been working in UX design field for more than 10 years at top companies, simply asking what they want to see in a junior UX designer's portfolio and how to make a portfolio stand out among other competitors. I also interviewed with a person who successfully made a transition from marketing to UX design. The questions I had for her were about how she presented her portfolio and how she entered this profession without previous related experience.

Result

• All of the interviewees and survey participants think having a portfolio showing the design process is extremely important.
• The survey shows that it is good to have three to six projects in the portfolio, but better not be more than that.
• The survey indicates that showing skillset is a must. The applicant should know popular design tools, such as Sketch, Adobe Suite, and Axure.
• Based on the survey, having the ability to code is a good thing, but it's not necessary.
• All of the interviewees said that the portfolio should be clear and easy to navigate.
• Two of the interviewees suggested to showcase the projects at the homepage, so the recruiter can see them right away.
